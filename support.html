Keep the observatory measuring

Palimpsest has no institutional funder. Every signal on this site — the deletion tracker, the Generative Firewall Index, the GDELT and refuge feeds — runs on infrastructure someone has to pay for. If the measurements are useful to you, a contribution keeps them running and makes them better: more vantage points, deeper archives, more models probed, faster refresh.

What a contribution funds

  • Vantage points. Differential observation needs eyes in more than one place. Network egress and residential vantage infrastructure is the single largest running cost.
  • Archival storage. Deletion as data only works if the original was archived first. Evidence snapshots grow every day and are never thrown away.
  • Model probes. The Generative Firewall Index pays per query to probe state-aligned models in two languages against control models.
  • Research-grade rigor. Human validation studies, uncertainty bands, and replication packaging so the data holds up in peer review, not just on a dashboard.

Contribute

Contributions are accepted in crypto only. That is deliberate: this project measures censors, and permissionless rails are the ones they cannot lean on. No donor list is kept and nothing is tracked.

Other ways to help

  • Cite the data. Palimpsest exists to be used. Cite it in research and journalism — method and raw evidence are on the data page.
  • Contribute code or validation. The project is MIT-licensed and developed in the open on GitHub. Human coders for the classifier validation study are especially welcome.
  • Spread the readings. Every share of a live reading makes the measurement harder to ignore and the commons harder to close.

Free and open-source, developed in the open as a public good. Contributions fund measurement infrastructure only — the project never monetizes the people or topics it observes. The internet stays free because people keep measuring the dark.
