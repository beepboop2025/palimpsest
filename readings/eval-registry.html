New · Verifiable Eval Registry

AI evaluations you can prove were not gamed

AI systems increasingly decide what people can and cannot learn, and the audits that check them can be quietly re-run, cherry-picked, or revised until the number looks right. This registry removes that move. The questions are frozen before any model is queried, and every result is hash-chained so it cannot be edited after the fact. Any model, any test suite. How it works

  1. Pre-register. The probe set is hashed and sealed into an append only chain before the model is ever asked.
  2. Run. Each result records the model, a hash of its full responses, and the metrics, and it must reference a probe set that was frozen earlier.
  3. Verify. Anyone recomputes the chain. A result whose questions were not frozen first, or any value altered after sealing, fails.
Why it matters: as models mediate more of what we can know, third parties need to audit them and prove the audit was not rewritten. A shared, independently verifiable record is the missing substrate. Palimpsest's own model-erasure audit is simply the first thing anchored here; the registry is model and topic agnostic.
Grounded in the trusted-public-archive and tamper-evident-logging literature (ARCHANGEL, arXiv:1804.08342; arXiv:2509.03821) and the model-censorship measurement work (arXiv:2505.17441, arXiv:2506.12349). The registry is stdlib only, key-less, and offline verifiable.  ·  raw JSON  ·  the chain
